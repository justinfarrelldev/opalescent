# Code of Conduct

Opalescent should be a place where people can learn, ask questions, and contribute without being belittled. Compiler and language-design work is difficult; patience matters.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful and constructive.
- Explain tradeoffs without attacking people.
- Assume good intent, especially from newcomers.
- Help beginners understand terminology instead of using it as a gatekeeping tool.
- Keep criticism focused on code, docs, design, and process.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, insults, or discriminatory language.
- Sexualized language or attention.
- Publishing private information without permission.
- Repeatedly derailing discussions after maintainers ask you to stop.
- Dismissing people because they are new to programming language design.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove comments; close issues; reject pull requests; or limit participation when behavior harms the community. Serious or repeated violations may result in a ban from project spaces.

If you need to report a conduct issue, contact the maintainers privately where possible. If no private contact is available yet, ask a maintainer for one without posting sensitive details publicly.
